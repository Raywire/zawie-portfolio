    Additionally, it is salient for me to mention that this passion of mine mentioned above was shaped at an early age as I grew up in Nairobi, the capital city of Kenya in Africa. Although I was privileged not to live in the slums of the city, my day-to-day life and activities made me interact with homeless and vulnerable children in the slums. It was normal for me to have regular meals and I could not reconcile the idea that some families could not have the regular meals or have a home. Occasionally I would with the help of my parents and the community church, donate clothes and other items that I had outgrown to the homeless and vulnerable children. Overtime, my desire to do more for these vulnerable families blossomed into a passion and a desire to understand the root causes of such disproportions in life and what I could do to make life better for the underprivileged in society.
